# Feature scaling
scaler = StandardScaler()
scaled_data = scaler.fit_transform(mall_customers[['Annual Income (k$)', 'Spending Score (1-100)']])

# Determine optimal number of clusters using the elbow method
wcss = []
for i in range(1, 11):
    kmeans = KMeans(n_clusters=i, init='k-means++', random_state=42)
    kmeans.fit(scaled_data)
    wcss.append(kmeans.inertia_)

# Plot the elbow curve
plt.figure(figsize=(8, 5))
plt.plot(range(1, 11), wcss, marker='o', linestyle='--')
plt.title('Elbow Method')
plt.xlabel('Number of Clusters')
plt.ylabel('WCSS (Within-Cluster Sum of Squares)')
plt.show()

# Fit K-means clustering algorithm with the optimal number of clusters
kmeans = KMeans(n_clusters=5, init='k-means++', random_state=42)
mall_customers['Cluster'] = kmeans.fit_predict(scaled_data)

# Visualize the clusters
plt.figure(figsize=(10, 6))
sns.scatterplot(data=mall_customers, x='Annual Income (k$)', y='Spending Score (1-100)', hue='Cluster', palette='viridis', legend='full')
plt.title('Customer Segmentation')
plt.xlabel('Annual Income (k$)')
plt.ylabel('Spending Score (1-100)')
plt.show()

Clustering analysis reveals segments where there are customers who earn relatively low incomes but still exhibit high spending behavior, as well as customers with high incomes who are conservative spenders. This complexity in customer behavior is precisely why clustering is valuable—it helps identify patterns and segments that might not be immediately obvious from simple demographic characteristics.

Here's what we're gaining from the clustering analysis despite these complexities:

  1. Segment Identification: Clustering helps identify these nuanced segments within the customer base. Even if some segments may not fit neatly into our preconceived notions based on income or spending behavior alone, clustering allows us to uncover these hidden patterns.

  2. Understanding Behavioral Patterns: By analyzing these clusters, businesses can gain deeper insights into the underlying behavioral patterns driving customer spending. For example, in segments where customers earn low incomes but still spend heavily, there might be factors like lifestyle choices, financial priorities, or even cultural influences at play.

  3. Targeted Marketing Opportunities: Despite the complexities, clustering still allows businesses to target specific customer segments with tailored marketing strategies. For instance, even if a segment contains customers with varying income and spending levels, there might be commonalities in their preferences or purchase motivations that businesses can leverage in their marketing efforts.

  4. Personalization and Customization: Clustering enables businesses to personalize their interactions and offerings for different segments. By understanding the unique characteristics and needs of each segment, businesses can deliver more relevant and targeted experiences, leading to higher customer satisfaction and loyalty.

  5. Optimizing Resource Allocation: Clustering helps optimize resource allocation by directing marketing resources, sales efforts, and product development towards the segments with the greatest potential for ROI. This ensures that limited resources are allocated efficiently to maximize impact and profitability.

In essence, while clustering may reveal complex and nuanced segments that defy simplistic categorization based on income or spending behavior alone, it still provides valuable insights that businesses can leverage to better understand their customers and tailor their strategies accordingly. By embracing this complexity and embracing the insights derived from clustering, businesses can gain a competitive edge in meeting the diverse needs and preferences of their customer base.
